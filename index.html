What's Happening at Matlock Bend

Republic Services is driving the bus — and Loudon County is along for the ride. Here's what we know.

📄

A Bad Contract

The 2022 Operating Agreement between Loudon County and Republic Services is poorly written, giving Republic significant control over expansion decisions while the county owns the property but has limited oversight.

Contract Issue
♻️

Zero Real Recycling

Loudon County currently recycles almost nothing. Republic charges recycling fees in surrounding counties, but those bins are mixed with regular garbage and dumped in the landfill. Only cardboard gets separated.

Environmental
☢️

Hazardous Waste Accepted

The landfill accepts PFAS, medical waste (often containing Teflon), and commercial nuclear/radioactive waste from hospitals within a 75-mile radius. There are no statistics, limits, or reporting requirements.

Public Health
🚛

Importing Other Counties' Worst Waste

Republic brings waste from up to 75 miles away to increase tipping fees. Other counties selectively ship their worst waste to Loudon County — not organics, but the hazardous and hard-to-process materials.

Inter-County
📏

26-Acre Expansion Pending

Republic is currently seeking TDEC permits for a 7-acre expansion, with an additional 19 acres available for future expansion. This increases capacity for decades more waste — including imports from other counties.

Expansion
🏛️

State Mandate Being Ignored

Tennessee has mandated that all counties reduce landfill inflow and increase recycling. Loudon County is not complying. Meanwhile, the entire state has only about 15 years of landfill capacity remaining.

Timeline of Events

Key milestones in the Matlock Bend Landfill story.

2007

Santek Operating Agreement Signed Contract

Original agreement between Loudon County Solid Waste Disposal Commission and Santek (later acquired by Republic Services).

2022

Second Amended Operating Agreement Contract

Current contract with Republic Services. Community members have identified issues with how it's written, giving Republic outsized control.

Nov 2024

Public Hearing on Expansion Expansion

Public hearing held for the proposed expansion. Strong community turnout from Tinsley National and surrounding areas.

Jan 2025

Notice of Violation Issued to Republic Violation

TDEC or LCSWDC issued a formal Notice of Violation to Republic Services for operational issues. Republic responded on January 31, 2025.

Jun–Jul 2025

TDEC Class I Inspections Permit

TDEC conducted multiple Class I inspections (June 9, July 21, July 29, 2025) for Permit SNL530000203. Reports detail compliance status.

2025–2026

7-Acre Expansion in Permitting Expansion

Republic Services is actively seeking TDEC approval for a 7-acre expansion, with 19 additional acres available for future phases.

Now

Community Organizing Begins Action

Residents are coming together to demand transparency, real recycling, and an end to hazardous waste acceptance. Meeting attendance has dropped — your presence matters.

Take Action

Three ways to make your voice heard. Being present is 90% of influence.

🗓️ Attend the Next Meeting

  1. Find the next LCSWDC meeting date at loudoncounty-tn.gov
  2. Show up in person. Online viewing doesn't count — physical presence is what moves the needle.
  3. Bring neighbors. Recent meetings had only 5-6 people. Fill the room.
  4. Ask questions. What waste is being accepted? Where are the recycling reports? Why is Republic expanding?

📞 Contact Your Representatives

  1. Call Rep. Monty Fritts — he sits on Agriculture & Natural Resources, the committee that handles waste policy. Ask him to support landfill reform.
  2. Email Sen. McNally — as Lt. Governor, he has statewide influence. Request a hearing on Tennessee's landfill capacity crisis.
  3. Contact TDEC — ask about the status of Permit SNL530000203 and what public comment opportunities exist.
  4. CC your county commissioners — they need to know residents are watching.

📋 Demand These Changes

  1. Ban PFAS acceptance in Tennessee landfills — it's not yet banned, but it should be.
  2. Separate processing for medical and radioactive waste — not dumped in the same landfill as household garbage.
  3. Mandatory recycling reporting — counties must report what percentage of waste is actually recycled.
  4. Treat waste as a utility — like electric, water, and sewer. Uniform statewide standards.
  5. Packaging regulations — reduce waste at the source by mandating recyclable materials.

📢 Spread the Word

  1. Share this website with neighbors, friends, and family in Loudon County.
  2. Post on social media — use hashtags like #LoudonCounty #MatlockBend #TNWaste
  3. Talk to your HOA — get neighborhood associations involved.
  4. Contact local media — WBIR, WATE, Knoxville News Sentinel. Reporters cover what communities care about.
  5. Join or form a community group — organized residents are harder to ignore than individuals.
